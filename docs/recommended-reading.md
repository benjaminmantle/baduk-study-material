# Recommended Reading

Two parts: the repo owner's **personal picks** (first-hand, battle-tested), then a **curated
canon** of the best books by topic and level with where to buy them. Books we host for free
(public-domain / freely released) live in **[books/](../books/)**; everything below that isn't
hosted is commercial — please buy it, the Go book world is tiny and worth supporting.

Legend: ⭐ = widely considered essential · 📗 = beginner · 📘 = DDK/SDK · 📙 = dan.

---

## The owner's personal picks

> *Preserved from the original collection — first-hand experience, not a stock list.*

I didn't do a lot of book study, but the books I did use extensively were the fun and effective
ones of the bunch. I had various Go clubs to go to and people to help me here and there, so along
with online play and a lot of time on my hands, I had what I needed to learn well. Books
definitely do help, and avoiding books is a bad plan.

- **Get Strong at the Opening / Get Strong at Attacking** — light and fun; after a read or two I
  was re-solving them entirely in under an hour. Great problem books.
- **Invincible: The Games of Shusaku** ⭐ — takes you through Shusaku's life story in an extremely
  entertaining way, each game led up to by the real events. Fully commented. What I gained most:
  opening direction and priorities, and overall Go sense — it put my opening far above other SDK
  players at the time. You don't even need the commentary; just play the games out and the
  recurring choices teach themselves.
- **Opening Theory Made Easy** — never read it myself but heard good things.
- **38 Basic Joseki** — important joseki for any aspiring 1-dan; very digestible, not intimidating.

## The canon, by topic & level

### Start here (complete beginner) 📗
- **Janice Kim — *Learn to Play Go* (Vols 1–5)** — the friendliest on-ramp in English.
- **Cho Chikun — *Go: A Complete Introduction to the Game*** — compact, authoritative.
- **Bozulich — *The Second Book of Go*** — the "what now?" after you know the rules.

### The one series to own 📘 ⭐
- **Elementary Go Series (Kiseido, 7 vols)** — the classic SDK backbone:
  1. *In the Beginning* (fuseki) · 2. *38 Basic Joseki* · 3. *Tesuji* ⭐ · 4. *Life and Death* ⭐ ·
  5. *Attack and Defense* ⭐ · 6. *The Endgame* · 7. *Handicap Go*.

### Life & death 📘📙
- **Davies — *Life and Death*** (Elem. Go v4) · **Cho Chikun — *Encyclopedia of Life & Death*** (reference) · **Bozulich — *Get Strong at Life and Death***.

### Tesuji 📘
- **Davies — *Tesuji*** ⭐ · **Bozulich — *Get Strong at Tesuji*** · **Sakata — *Tesuji and Anti-Suji***.

### Opening / direction 📘📙
- **Otake — *Opening Theory Made Easy*** ⭐ (the owner's pick) · **Kajiwara — *The Direction of Play*** 📙 · **Ishigure — *In the Beginning***.

### Joseki 📘📙
- **Kosugi/Davies — *38 Basic Joseki*** · **Get Strong at Joseki 1–3** · **Kogo's Joseki Dictionary** (free — [hosted here](../05-joseki/)) · **Ishida — *Dictionary of Basic Joseki* (3 vols)** 📙.

### Shape 📘
- **Van Zeijst & Bozulich — *Making Good Shape*** ⭐ · **Matthews & Kim — *Shape Up!*** (free PDF; see [06-shape](../06-shape/)).

### Attack, defense & fighting 📘📙
- **Ishida/Davies — *Attack and Defense*** ⭐ · **Get Strong at Attacking** (owner's pick) · **Shirae — *Fight Like a Pro***.

### Endgame 📘📙
- **Ogawa/Davies — *The Endgame*** · **Get Strong at the Endgame** · **Berlekamp & Wolfe — *Mathematical Go*** 📙 (advanced).

### Positional judgment 📙
- **Cho Chikun — *Positional Judgment*** ⭐ — how to count and steer the whole board.

### Whole-game collections & appreciation 📙
- **Power — *Invincible: The Games of Shusaku*** ⭐ · **Go Seigen — *A Way of Play for the 21st Century*** · **Appreciating Famous Games** · **Modern Master Games** series · **An Younggil — *Relentless: Lee Sedol vs Gu Li*** (a copy is [hosted here](../books/)).

### Where to buy
- **Kiseido** — kiseido.com (publisher of the Elementary Go Series)
- **Slate & Shell** — slateandshell.com
- **Gobooks / SmartGo Books** — app with many titles interactive
- **Yellow Mountain Imports**, **Amazon** — equipment + books
