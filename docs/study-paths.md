# Study Paths

How to actually get stronger with the material in this repo — ordered routes by level, with
rough time budgets. The recurring theme of Go improvement is boring but true:

> **Play games. Do life & death every day. Review your losses. Repeat.**

Everything else accelerates that loop; nothing replaces it.

Rank shorthand: **Beginner** ≈ 30k–20k · **DDK** 20k–10k · **SDK** 9k–1k · **Dan** 1d+.

---

## 🟢 Beginner (≈30k–20k) — "I know the rules"

Goal: stop losing whole groups, start seeing territory.

1. **[01 Rules & Basics](../01-rules-basics/)** — capturing, connection, the two-eye rule, ko.
2. **Very easy [tsumego](../02-life-and-death/graded/)** — 20–30 *easy* problems a day. They should be almost too easy; speed and pattern recognition matter more than difficulty now.
3. **Play 9×9, then 13×13.** Small boards teach fighting and life & death fast without drowning you in whole-board strategy.
4. Learn **[a handful of proverbs](../12-proverbs-principles/)** — they're training wheels that work.

Skip joseki, fuseki theory, and endgame for now. Reading + playing is 90% of your gains here.

---

## 🔵 DDK (20k–10k) — "I can keep groups alive, mostly"

Goal: whole-board thinking; stop giving away the opening.

- **Daily:** 20–30 [graded tsumego](../02-life-and-death/graded/) (easy→medium). This never stops being the highest-ROI habit.
- **[04 Opening principles](../04-opening-fuseki/)** — corners → sides → center; approach, extend, don't crawl on the 2nd line.
- **[03 Tesuji](../03-tesuji/)** — start the basic tactical patterns (cut, atari, net, ladder, clamp).
- **Move to 19×19** and play regularly. **Review every loss** — just find the *one* move where it went wrong.
- Light **[11 handicap](../11-handicap/)** reading if you play handicap games.

---

## 🟣 SDK (9k–1k) — "I understand the game, I want to break into dan"

Goal: reading depth, tactical vocabulary, and not throwing away points.

- **Daily tsumego, harder:** [collections](../02-life-and-death/collections/) like *360 Basic Tsumego*, then step up. Re-solve sets until they're instant.
- **[03 Tesuji](../03-tesuji/)** properly — this is the difference-maker for SDK→dan.
- **[05 Joseki](../05-joseki/)** — learn a *few* common ones and, crucially, *why* each move is played and how to punish mistakes. Don't memorize dictionaries.
- **[08 Endgame](../08-endgame-yose/)** — even rough move-value sense wins games you're "losing." Wildly underrated.
- **[10 Whole games](../10-whole-games/)** — play through pro games quickly (don't over-analyze); absorb shape and direction by osmosis. Classic games (Shusaku) are great for opening sense.
- **[07 Fighting](../07-middlegame-fighting/)** — invasions, reductions, and capturing races (semeai) counting.

---

## 🔴 Dan (1d+) — "I want to keep climbing"

Goal: precision, judgment, and modern (AI-informed) understanding.

- **[Special shapes](../02-life-and-death/special-shapes/)** — L-group, J-group, carpenter's square, comb, bent-four, and the life & death that arises straight out of joseki. Know these cold; they decide real games.
- **[09 Positional judgment](../09-positional-judgment/)** — count the board. Territory, thickness value, who's ahead, what the position needs.
- **[10 AI-era games](../10-whole-games/ai-era/)** — AlphaGo/KataGo reshaped opening & joseki theory. Study what changed.
- **[13 Study with AI](../13-ai-and-tools/)** — review your own games with KataGo/KaTrain. Find your *recurring* mistakes, not one-off blunders.
- Deep **[endgame](../08-endgame-yose/)** (tedomari, sente/gote value, temperature) and **[joseki](../05-joseki/)** choice by whole-board fit.

## The universal weekly loop (any level)

| Day-to-day | Why |
|---|---|
| **Tsumego every day** (even 10 min) | Reading is the engine of strength at every rank. |
| **Play real games** (correct time settings) | You only internalize by deciding under pressure. |
| **Review losses — one key move** | Fix causes, not symptoms. Don't drown in analysis. |
| **One pro game, played fast** | Trains shape, direction, and "what good looks like." |
| **A little of your weakest topic** | Endgame and fighting are the usual hidden leaks. |
